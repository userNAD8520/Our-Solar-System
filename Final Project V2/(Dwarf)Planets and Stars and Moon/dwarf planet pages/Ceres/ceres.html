Fact's about Ceres

ASTEROID ACCLAIM

Ceres, first discovered in 1801, is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.

DUAL DESIGNATION

It's unique in being both the largest asteroid and the only dwarf planet located in the inner solar system.

WATER WEALTH

Ceres has more fresh water than Earth, most of it frozen in a layer beneath the surface, potentially forming a sub-surface ocean.

BRIGHT BLEMISHES

It has mysterious bright spots in its craters, most notably in Occator Crater, believed to be a type of salt.

CRATER COLLECTION

Ceres is covered in countless craters of varying sizes, evidence of numerous impacts over billions of years.

DAWN'S DESTINATION

NASA's Dawn spacecraft orbited and studied Ceres from 2015 to 2018, providing detailed images and data.

ORGANIC OFFERINGS

In 2017, scientists announced the discovery of organic molecules on Ceres, essential ingredients for life as we know it.

YEARLY YAW

It takes Ceres about 4.6 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.
