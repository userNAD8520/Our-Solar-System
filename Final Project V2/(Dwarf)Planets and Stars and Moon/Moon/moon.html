Lunar Chronicles

Earth's Moon is the only place beyond Earth where humans have set foot.

The brightest and largest object in our night sky, the Moon makes Earth a more livable planet by moderating our home planet's wobble on its axis, leading to a relatively stable climate. It also causes tides, creating a rhythm that has guided humans for thousands of years. The Moon was likely formed after a Mars-sized body collided with Earth several billion years ago.

Earth's Moon is the fifth largest of the 200+ moons orbiting planets in our solar system. Earth's only natural satellite is simply called "the Moon" because people didn't know other moons existed until Galileo Galilei discovered four moons orbiting Jupiter in 1610.

Fact's about the Earth's Moon

FORMATION

The Moon formed from debris after a Mars-sized body collided with Earth around 4.5 billion years ago.

NO ATMOSPHERE

The Moon lacks an atmosphere, leading to a windless and weatherless lunar environment.

GRAVITY

The Moon's gravity is only one-sixth that of Earth's, causing lower weight for objects there.

SYNCHRONOUS ROTATION

Due to synchronous rotation, only one side of the Moon is visible from Earth.

MOONQUAKES

The Moon experiences moonquakes, similar to earthquakes, due to gravitational interactions with Earth.

TIDAL INFLUENCE

The Moon's gravitational pull significantly influences Earth's tides.

PRESERVED FOOTPRINTS

Footprints from Apollo astronauts are preserved on the Moon's surface due to the lack of atmosphere and erosion.

HUMAN VISIT

The Apollo 11 mission landed the first humans, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin, on the Moon in 1969.
