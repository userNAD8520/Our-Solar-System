Uranus Chronicles

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, and has the third-largest diameter in our solar system. It was the first planet found with the aid of a telescope.

Uranus was discovered in 1781 by astronomer William Herschel, although he originally thought it was either a comet or a star. It was two years later that the object was universally accepted as a new planet, in part because of observations by astronomer Johann Elert Bode.

Herschel tried unsuccessfully to name his discovery Georgium Sidus after King George III. Instead, the scientific community accepted Bode's suggestion to name it Uranus, the Greek god of the sky, as suggested by Bode.

Fact's about Uranus

SIDLE SIDEWAYS

Unique among planets, Uranus has a sideways tilt, causing it to spin nearly on its side.

ICE IDENTITY

Classified as an ice giant, Uranus is composed largely of ices, like water, ammonia, and methane.

MYSTERIOUS MAGNETISM

Its magnetic field is tilted and uneven, causing a complex and unusual magnetosphere.

MOONS' MONIKERS

Uranus has 27 known moons, all named after characters from the works of William Shakespeare and Alexander Pope.

RINGED RARITY

Its delicate and faint ring system was the second to be discovered in the Solar System, after Saturn's.

ORBITAL ODYSSEY

It takes Uranus approximately 84 Earth years to orbit the Sun.

POLAR PHENOMENON

Its poles get 42 years of continuous sunlight and darkness due to its extreme tilt.

GASEOUS GLOW

Uranus appears as a blue-green disk due to the absorption of red light by atmospheric methane.
