Venus Chronicles

Venus, Earth's closest planetary neighbor, is the second planet from the Sun and one of the four terrestrial planets. Often called Earth's twin, Venus shares similar size and density with Earth, but that's where the similarities end.

The atmosphere on Venus is vastly different. Filled with carbon dioxide and shrouded in thick, yellowish sulfuric acid clouds, this toxic environment creates a strong greenhouse effect. It’s the hottest planet in our solar system, surpassing even Mercury, which is closer to the Sun.

Surface temperatures on Venus can reach 900 degrees Fahrenheit (475 degrees Celsius), capable of melting lead. The surface is a rusty color, featuring crunched mountains and thousands of large volcanoes, some of which scientists believe could still be active.

Fact's about Venus

RETROGRADE RARITY

Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most other planets, a phenomenon known as retrograde rotation.

VOLCANIC VISTA

It's home to more volcanoes than any other planet in our solar system, with over 1,600 confirmed.

CLIMATE CALAMITY

Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system due to a runaway greenhouse effect.

PRESSURIZED PRESENCE

It has a surface pressure 92 times that of Earth, similar to being about 900 meters underwater.

CLOUDY CANOPY

Its thick, toxic atmosphere consists mainly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid.

ORBITAL ODDITY

Venus' day is longer than its year, taking 243 Earth days to rotate once and 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun.

TWIN TRAITS

Venus is often called Earth's 'sister planet' due to their similar sizes, gravities, and rocky compositions.

LUMINOUS LOOK

It's the brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon, often referred to as the 'morning star' or 'evening star'.
