Sun Chronicles

Our Sun is a 4.5 billion-year-old star, a glowing orb of hydrogen and helium at the heart of our solar system. Located about 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) from Earth, its energy is indispensable to life as we know it on our home planet.

The Sun dominates our solar system as its largest object - it would take 1.3 million Earths to fill its volume. Its gravitational pull binds the solar system, maintaining everything from the largest planets to the smallest debris in orbit around it.

Within the Sun's core, temperatures exceed 27 million degrees Fahrenheit (15 million degrees Celsius), making it the hottest part of the Sun. The Sun's activity, from potent eruptions to the continuous stream of charged particles it emits, shapes the character of space throughout the solar system.

Fact's about the Sun

LUMINOUS LIFELINE

The Sun, a 4.5 billion-year-old star, is vital for life on Earth, providing the energy that drives climate and weather.

GASEOUS GIANT

It's primarily composed of hydrogen and helium gases.

SOLAR SOVEREIGN

With a diameter over 100 times that of Earth, it's the largest object in our solar system.

GRAVITATIONAL GLUE

Its immense gravity keeps the entire solar system, from planets to asteroids, in their respective orbits.

CORE COMBUSTION

The Sun's core reaches temperatures over 27 million degrees Fahrenheit (15 million degrees Celsius), fueling nuclear fusion.

RADIANT RHYTHM

It operates on an 11-year cycle, going through periods of high and low solar activity known as the solar maxima and minima.

MAGNETIC MAJESTY

The Sun's magnetic field creates impressive solar flares and coronal mass ejections that can affect Earth's technology.

SOLAR WIND SAILOR

It constantly emits a stream of charged particles known as the solar wind, which can reach Earth and cause auroras.
